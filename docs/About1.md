# Why I Built This
  
I'm Omar Peracha. I built this because of a problem I saw while answering phone calls for help.
  
After 27 years in finance at Chevron and then Shell, I retired. A few years of travel and long-distance hiking later, I started looking for volunteer work and joined the Society of St. Vincent de Paul and the Christian Community Service Center. Both organizations take calls from people seeking financial assistance. When someone called whose need fell outside our service area, all we had was a paper list of referrals — and that was often where the search ended.
  
I'd always been drawn to coding. Through my career I built tools that made my own job easier.  Due to the rigidity of the companies, I sometimes had to do this surreptitiously, often falling back on the old adage of asking forgiveness rather than permission. So I started a Google Sheet with about 100 of the most-used referrals. It grew. To keep it manageable, it had to get more interactive: data validation, then scripts, then a real application with its own database.
  
I'm now on version 8 - and these days the actual coding is done by Claude Code, while I focus on design, product decisions and maintenance of the third party service providers. I check in periodically with the largest organizations to keep their information current. The site is free, has no ads, and exists for one reason: when someone reaches out for help, hitting a dead end shouldn't be where the search ends.
  
## What's in the Guide
  
The directory contains over 1,000 resources from 526 organizations across the Greater Houston Area — nonprofits, government agencies, faith-based organizations, and community programs. When an organization offers more than one type of help, each is its own entry, so a single organization can appear as several resources.
  
Each listing shows contact details, hours of operation, eligibility requirements, the zip codes the resource serves, distance from your search zip code, and a current status (active, limited, inactive, or closed). Organizations are never removed from the directory — when a program closes, the entry stays, marked as closed, so nothing is silently missing.
  
Coverage spans:
  
- 14 counties
- 136 cities
- 284 zip codes
- 30 categories of assistance, from emergency food to long-term housing
  
## Who It's For
  
The directory is built for two audiences:
  
- **Clients in need** — Anyone in the Greater Houston Area looking for community resources for themselves or someone they care for. Available without an account, no signup required.
- **Caseworkers, navigators, and partner organizations** — Social workers, case managers, hospital discharge planners, school counselors, and others who help clients access services. Registered organizations get additional features: emailing or texting customized resource lists to clients, and generating PDF handouts.
